What is Climate Change?

Climate change: the defining issue of our time. The term climate change refers to the gradual increase in the Earth's temperature over the past couple of centuries. While the Earth's temperature has risen and fallen naturally over the course of its existence, the rise we are currently experiencing is proven to be caused by human activity. The warming of the earth does not just mean our summers are hotter. The natural world we occupy is a remarkably delicate and balanced scale after millions of years of evolution across millions of species. The slightest tip, such as higher temperatures or rising sea levels, can throw this entire system off balance and put the condition of our ecosystems in danger: ecosystems that you and I rely on every single day.

Feasible, Worthwhile Individual Action for the Present.

With regards to environmentalism, many fall into pitfalls of despair and apathy. Striving to take the smallest, easiest changes is the most feasible way for an individual to have an effective and positive impact. It is unrealistic to expect one individual to be a hero and adopt a radically different lifestyle. This isn't necessary yet: these small individual actions, when performed as a collective, significant change will be brought about. After all, it is for the sake of future generations. Effective Policy Shaping for the Future

Collaborative effort through our own individual action is great, but some of the most effective change will come through policy. 100 corporations account for over 70% of worldwide emissions, most of them dealing in oil or natural gas. It is on governments to hold them accountable and demand environmentally sustainable practice. Due to action like the Paris Agreements, companies have signaled to adopt such practices. Tax subsidies to renewables and electric vehicles, such as those from the Inflation Reduction Act, will expedite the phase-out of nonrenewables, the source of the problem in the first place. This page will show how governments, from the federal to local level, have contributed and further action that can be taken.
